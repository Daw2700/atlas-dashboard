How to read this

Everything here is paper money: a hypothetical $5,000 account where every pick risks 1% of current equity. The rules — what qualifies as a bet, every threshold, every model version — were written down before the results, and changes are logged publicly below. The backtest replay is in-sample evidence rendered as it would have looked live; the pre-registered holdout, not the replay, decides whether the model survives. Most days the live system fires nothing, because most markets don't offer an edge worth 2× the cost of trading them. The records are append-only and no verdict is drawn before 100 resolved live picks.
